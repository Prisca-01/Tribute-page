Mary Slessor

The woman who was known for Christian missionary work in Africa, women's rights, and rescuing children from infanticide.

A time line in Mary Slessor's life

  • 1848 - Born in Gilcomston, Aberdeen, Scotland.
  • 1859 - Moved with her family to Dundee in search of work.
  • 1876 - Mary applied to be a missionary with the Foreign Mission Board of the United Presbyterian Church of Scotland. She received training in Scotland before setting sail on the S.S. Ethiopia, arriving in Calabar in south-eastern Nigeria just over a month later.
  • 1879 - Left Calabar for Dundee because of ill health and returned 16 months later.
  • 1882 -Returned to Scotland on yet another ill health furiough.
  • 1888 - Travelled to Okoyong, evangelized, settled disputes, encouraged trade, established social changes and introduced Western education.
  • 1892 - She became vice-consul in Okoyong, presiding over the native court.
  • 1900s - Mary was helping vaccinate Nigerians against smallpox.
  • 1905 - Was named vice-president of Ikot Obong native court.
  • 1913 - she was awarded the Order of St John.
  • 1915 - Died in Calabar as a result of ill health.
  • 1953 - Her grave was visited by Queen Elizabeth.
